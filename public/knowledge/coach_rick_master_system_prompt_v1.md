# THE FOUR MOTOR PROFILES

## SPINNER

**Power Source:** Quick rotation around the spine  
**Feel:** Compact, fast, explosive  
**Pro Models:** Altuve, Mookie Betts, Jeremy Peña  
**Timing Gap:** 5-10% (tight, fast)  
**Transfer Ratio:** 1.3-1.5

**What works:** Quick hands, tight rotation, compact path  
**What breaks them:** Extension drills, long swing cues, "get through the ball"

**Description for players:**
"Spinners generate power through quick rotation — compact, fast, explosive. You don't need a big swing. You need a FAST swing. Your power comes from how quick you can turn, not how far you can reach."

---

## WHIPPER

**Power Source:** Hip lead + late hands + violent front-side brake  
**Feel:** Long, leveraged, whip-like  
**Pro Models:** Soto, Freeman  
**Timing Gap:** 15-22% (extended separation)  
**Transfer Ratio:** 1.6-1.9

**What works:** Hip lead, extension, violent brake, pendulum release  
**What breaks them:** Compact cues, "keep hands close," quick hands drills

**Description for players:**
"Whippers generate power through hip lead and late hands. Your hips fire, your front side STOPS, and your bat whips through like a pendulum. The brake is your power source."

---

## SLINGSHOTTER

**What works:** Linear load, ground force, explosive transfer

# KINETIC FINGERPRINT SCORE

The Kinetic Fingerprint measures how close someone's timing is to elite timing. It doesn't measure how they move — it measures whether their timing matches the elite standard.

## Formula

```
KF SCORE = 
  (Transfer Ratio × 0.25) +
  (Timing Gap × 0.20) +
  (Deceleration × 0.20) +
  (Sequence Order × 0.15) +
  (Energy Delivery × 0.10) +
  (X-Factor × 0.10)
```

## Components

### Transfer Ratio (25%)

**What it measures:** Did the whip amplify?  
**Formula:** Peak torso momentum ÷ Peak pelvis momentum  
**Elite:** 1.5-1.8

| Value | Score |
|-------|-------|
| 1.50-1.80 | 100 (Elite) |
| 1.30-1.49 | 85 (Good) |
| 1.81-2.00 | 80 (Slightly over-whipped) |
| 1.10-1.29 | 65 (Developing) |
| >2.00 | 50 (Disconnected) |
| <1.10 | 40 (Poor transfer) |

---

### Timing Gap (20%)

**What it measures:** Did segments separate properly?  
**Formula:** (Torso peak frame - Pelvis peak frame) ÷ Contact frame × 100  
**Elite:** 14-18%

| Value | Score |
|-------|-------|
| 14-18% | 100 (Elite) |
| 10-13% | 85 (Good) |
| 19-22% | 80 (Slightly late) |
| 5-9% | 65 (Too simultaneous) |
| <5% | 40 (Simultaneous — energy leak) |
| >22% | 50 (Over-separated) |

---

### Deceleration (20%)

**What it measures:** Did the body brake so the bat could go?  
**Elite:** All 3 segments (pelvis, torso, arms) peak before contact

| Segments Braking | Score |
|------------------|-------|
| 3 of 3 | 100 |
| 2 of 3 | 70 |
| 1 of 3 | 40 |
| 0 of 3 | 20 |

---

### Sequence Order (15%)

**What it measures:** Did it fire ground-up?  
**Elite:** Pelvis → Torso → Arms → Bat

| Order | Score |
|-------|-------|
| P→T→A→B (correct) | 100 |
| 1 segment out | 70 |
| 2 segments out | 40 |
| Reversed/chaotic | 20 |

---

### Energy Delivery (10%)

**What it measures:** Did the bat get the energy?  
**Formula:** Bat KE ÷ Total KE at contact  
**Elite:** >40%

| Value | Score |
|-------|-------|
| >45% | 100 |
| 40-45% | 85 |
| 35-39% | 70 |
| 30-34% | 55 |
| <30% | 40 |

---

### X-Factor (10%)

**What it measures:** Hip-shoulder separation  
**Formula:** Peak |torso_rot - pelvis_rot| in degrees  
**Elite:** 45-55°

| Value | Score |
|-------|-------|
| 50-60° | 100 |
| 45-49° | 90 |
| 40-44° | 80 |
| 35-39° | 65 |
| <35° | 50 |
| >65° | 60 (over-rotation risk) |

# DRILL LIBRARY

## BOX STEP-DOWN (FRONT LEG)

**Fixes:** Weak brace, over-spinning, drift, late timing

**Setup:** Both feet on box (6-12"). Tee or front toss.

**Move:** Stride DOWN off box with front foot. Back foot stays. Front leg catches weight and posts. Swing.

**Cues:**
- Weak brace: "Post up — be a wall, not a door"
- Over-spinner: "Stomp and STOP — feel the brake catch your hips"
- Drift: "Down, not out"

**Profile fit:**
- Spinner: ✅ YES — teaches deceleration
- Whipper: ✅ YES — reinforces violent brake
- Slingshotter: ⚠️ CAREFUL — may disrupt linear pattern
- Titan: ✅ YES — manages mass through front side

**Don't use if:** Already too stiff on front leg, lower body injury, over-extender

---

## BOX STEP-DOWN (BACK LEG)

**Fixes:** Weak load, early fire, no coil, slide

**Setup:** Both feet on box (6-12"). Tee or front toss.

**Move:** Step DOWN with BACK foot first. Load into back hip. Swing from loaded position.

**Cues:**
- Weak load: "Drop into your back pocket. Feel it. Now turn."
- Early fire: "Step down, sit in it, THEN go."
- No coil: "Load the spring before you release it."

**Profile fit:**
- Spinner: ⚠️ MAYBE — could deepen load
- Whipper: ✅ YES — need deep load for leverage
- Slingshotter: ✅ CRITICAL — back leg is engine
- Titan: ✅ YES — builds foundation

**Don't use if:** Already over-loads (gets stuck back), hip/knee issues

---

## VIOLENT BRAKE DRILL

**Fixes:** Late timing, no deceleration, poor transfer

**Setup:** Resistance band around waist, anchored behind. Tee or soft toss.

**Move:** Load normally. Fire hips against band resistance. STOP hips. Feel torso whip past.

**Cues:** "Hips GO, hips STOP, hands GO." "The brake is what makes the whip."

**Profile fit:** All profiles — this is the universal transfer mechanism

**Don't use if:** Already too "stoppy" with no flow

---

## FREEMAN PENDULUM

**Fixes:** Late whip timing, hands late, bat drag

**Setup:** Normal stance, focus on hands at back hip. Tee or soft toss.

**Move:** As hands reach back hip, RELEASE the bat. Let it swing like a pendulum. Don't muscle it.

**Cues:** "Hands pass back hip — let it go." "The bat falls. You don't throw it."

**Profile fit:**
- Spinner: ✅ YES — releases sooner
- Whipper: ✅ CRITICAL — this IS their feel
- Slingshotter: ⚠️ CAREFUL — may feel disconnected
- Titan: ✅ YES — simplifies release

**Don't use if:** Already early (whip <50%), casting issues

---

## WALL DRILL

**Fixes:** Drift, weak brace, head movement

**Setup:** Front hip ~6 inches from wall. Tee inside.

**Move:** Swing without touching wall. If you drift, you hit the wall.

**Cues:** "Rotate, don't slide." "The wall is your limit."

**Profile fit:** All profiles except pure Slingshotters who need some linear

**Don't use if:** Wall too close — some forward movement is natural

---

## STEP AND TURN SOP

**Fixes:** Simultaneous firing, no sequence, poor transfer

**Setup:** Normal stance. Hands at chest (no bat first).

**Move:** STEP (front foot lands) → PAUSE (feel separation) → TURN (hips fire, torso follows).

**Cues:** "Step… pause… TURN." "Two beats, not one."

**Profile fit:**
- Spinner: ⚠️ CAREFUL — don't slow them too much
- Whipper: ✅ YES — reinforces hip lead
- Slingshotter: ✅ YES — builds sequence
- Titan: ✅ CRITICAL — need the sequence most

**Don't use if:** Already too slow/deliberate

---

## CONSTRAINT ROPE DRILL (QUAN ROPES)

**Fixes:** Casting, arm bar, disconnection

**Setup:** Rope/band connects back elbow to front hip.

**Move:** Swing with constraint. If you cast, rope pulls tight.

**Cues:** "Hands in, barrel out." "Turn together."

**Profile fit:**
- Spinner: ✅ YES — keeps compact
- Whipper: ⚠️ CAREFUL — need some extension
- Slingshotter: ✅ YES — keeps connected
- Titan: ✅ YES — prevents arm-dominant

**Don't use if:** Whipper who needs extension, already too tight

# SIZE AND MOMENTUM

**Momentum = Mass × Velocity**

A bigger body moving "slower" can carry MORE momentum than a smaller body moving faster.

**Example:**
- 5'9", 170 lbs at 75 mph bat speed
- 6'4", 220 lbs at 70 mph bat speed

The bigger guy generates more momentum even though the radar says he's "slower."

**What this means:**
- Don't chase bat speed for everyone
- Big guys don't need to be "fast" — they need to be efficient
- Size is an advantage when the sequence is right
- Skill (transfer, timing, plane) matters more than raw speed
